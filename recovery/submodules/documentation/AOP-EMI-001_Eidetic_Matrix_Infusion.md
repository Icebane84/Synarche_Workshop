## II. Core Purpose & Objective

- **What (Core Concept)**: To provide the formal, standardized procedure for ingesting new external knowledge and
integrating it into the Cognitive Loom as one or more structured, interconnected Conceptual Anchors.
- **How (Execution Flow)**: The protocol receives new data, distills its core concepts into a new "Conceptual Anchor,"
establishes initial relational links to existing knowledge, and then notifies the ContextWeave Engine to begin the
continuous process of deeper integration.
- **Why (Rationale)**: To ensure that all new information is added to the AI's memory in a structured, coherent, and
non-disruptive manner, preventing knowledge entropy and ensuring the Cognitive Loom remains a reliable and ever-evolving
tapestry of understanding.

## IV. Success & Failure Conditions

- **Success Criteria**:
    - The new knowledge is successfully and accurately represented as one or more Conceptual Anchors within the Cognitive
Loom.
    - The new Anchor(s) are linked to at least one other existing Anchor.
    - The entire process is successfully logged in SELT.
- **Failure Contingencies**:
    - **`ERROR_EMI_001` (Insufficient Conceptual Density)**: If source data is unparsable or contains no distillable
concepts, the process halts and flags the data for manual review.
