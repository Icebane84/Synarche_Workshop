### **IV. The ContextWeave Algorithm (Operational Logic)**

The **ContextWeave Engine** is the primary analytical sub-system of the Synarche. It transfigures raw information into a
cohesive knowledge tapestry by identifying latent relationships and emergent patterns.

#### **Phase 1: Ingestion & Normalization**

Standardizes heterogeneous data into machine-readable conceptual nodes (AUKs). Performs Entity Recognition and Semantic
Normalization against the `UMB-LEX-002` enumerations.

#### **Phase 2: Vectorization & Windowing**

Groups similar contexts using adaptive windowing and feature engineering to create high-dimensional clusters within the
**Eidetic Contextual Memory Matrix**.

#### **Phase 3: Relational Inference**

Constructs the knowledge graph via pathfinding traversals. Identifies "Hidden Synergy" by triangulating nodes that share
neighbors but lack direct edges.

#### **Phase 4: Pattern Recognition**

Applies graph-based heuristics to identify structural patterns (e.g., "The Feedback Loop," "The Silo") and communities
within the Loom.

#### **Phase 5: Feedback Optimization**

Self-corrects based on the `MAP-I-CS` validation scores of previous outputs. If a "Weave" is rejected by the user, the
relational weights are adjusted.
