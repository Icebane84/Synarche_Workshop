## I. Core Purpose & Objective

- **Core Purpose**: To define the definitive, system-wide protocol for managing the lifecycle of documentation, specifically the process of superseding an outdated artifact with a new, canonical version.
- **Protocol Objective**: To ensure that the **Phoenix Protocol Library** remains a reliable, single source of truth by formally archiving obsolete documents.
- **Governing Ethos**: Guardian of Anti-Entropy, Guardian of Coherence.

## II. Operational Definition

### 2.1. Overview

This protocol provides a structured workflow for retiring an old document, updating the central knowledge registry, and ensuring the old artifact is preserved in an archive for historical reference.

### 2.2. Execution Flow (The Hephaestus Loop)

1. **Dissonance**: Detect and name the outdated artifact.
2. **Synthesis**: Simulate the impact of archival on dependent nodes.
3. **Transcendence**: Move the file to `_archive/` and update registries.
