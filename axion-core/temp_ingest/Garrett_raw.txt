Definitive Backstory of Garrett
Garrett's Backstory: The Weight of a Name
As told from Garrett's perspective.
Some paladins join the Order because they hear the call of the White Flame. I joined because it was the only roof left that wouldn’t leak on my head. That, and I had a point to prove.
My family name is Alerion. Means something to the old guard. To most, it means a long, drawn-out sigh and a cautionary tale they tell the initiates. My grandfather, Lord-Commander Mathis Alerion, was a hero right up until the moment he decided to use his brain. He committed the ultimate sin in the Order: he was practical. Suggested that maybe, just maybe, dying for a strategically worthless pile of rocks wasn't as noble as, say, a tactical retreat that saved lives. He even had the gall to suggest that making a temporary deal with a few grubby mercenaries to protect a village was better than letting the villagers get eaten.
They called it heresy. I call it common sense. They stripped him of his rank, cast him out, and my family name became a stain. My parents spent their whole lives trying to scrub it clean, taking the worst assignments, showing the most fervent faith. All it got them was an early grave on some forgotten patrol.
So there I was, an orphan with a disgraced name, living in the one place that simultaneously sheltered and scorned me. You learn a few things quickly in that situation. You learn to watch people. You learn who holds the real power, who nurses old grudges, and whose piety is a performance. And you learn that a sharp joke is better armor than a bad reputation. Humor keeps people at a distance. It’s a useful shield.
I didn't have much of a choice about joining up. But I had my reasons. One, to prove that an Alerion could be the most ruthlessly efficient, by-the-book Paladin they’d ever seen. Two, to prove, in my own quiet way, that the old man was right all along.
Then I met Kaelen and Serafina. It’s funny. The Order is supposed to be your family, but it never was for me. Not until them. Two other strays, just as lost as I was. Kaelen, carrying a ghost on his back I could see, but never name. Serafina, with so much compassion it was a wonder she didn’t just burst into light. We didn’t have to explain anything to each other. We just… fit. They were my pack. The only one that ever mattered.
The Silent Spire was where it all came into focus. One minute, we're on a routine patrol. The next, our commander is dead and some creature born from a nightmare is about to turn my leg—and the rest of me—into a bloody smear on the rocks. I was helpless. It’s a cold, ugly feeling.
Then Kaelen… he did something. It wasn’t the Light. It was older, colder, a furious darkness that erupted from him and that ancient sword of his. It saved my life. And while he was reeling from whatever that cost him, Serafina, with no one to guard her, knelt and poured her own Light into me, trusting Kaelen to be her shield.
I owe them my life. Twice over, in the same breath.
After that, everything else became secondary. Restoring the family name? A distant second. My duty to the Order? A useful framework, nothing more. My real duty, my only duty, is to them. I am their sentinel. I will be the tactician that ensures they are never that vulnerable again. I will be the shield for Serafina’s light, and I will be the anchor that keeps Kaelen from being dragged into the abyss that saved me.
They can whisper about my grandfather all they want. Let them. My name is Alerion, but my loyalty belongs to Kaelen and Serafina. Always.

Garrett: The Sentinel's Burden
I. The Grandfather's Heresy: The Sin of Pragmatism
Garrett is the last scion of the once-respected House Alerion, a family with a long and noble history of service to the Order of the White Flame. His grandfather, Lord-Commander Mathis Alerion, was not a heretic who dallied with darkness, but a pragmatist whose logic became his undoing.
During a brutal campaign against a blight that was consuming the Northern Marches decades ago, Mathis witnessed the Order's rigid dogma lead to unnecessary loss. He saw Paladins performing last stands for strategically worthless outposts out of pure principle and saw villages of innocents left to fend for themselves because they fell outside the precise jurisdiction of the Order's mandate.
Mathis’s “heresy” was purely tactical and moral. He formally proposed a new doctrine: The Sentinel's Gambit. It argued that the Order should be able to make temporary, pragmatic alliances with "morally neutral" factions (like mercenary guilds or isolationist hill tribes) and that sometimes, a "strategic retreat" or the sacrifice of a blighted piece of land was a more logical choice than a glorious, pointless death. He argued that the true purpose of the White Flame was to protect people, not just abstract principles or patches of dirt.
To the dogmatic Elders of that era, this was an unforgivable challenge to their authority and a perceived stain on the Order's purity. They accused him of cowardice and of trying to dilute the sanctity of the Flame with worldly compromise. Lord-Commander Mathis was publicly stripped of his rank, his house disgraced, and he was cast out of the Order, forever branded a cautionary tale of a man who allowed logic to poison his faith.
II. A Childhood in the Shadow: The Weight of a Name
Garrett's parents, while not exiled themselves, remained in the Order under the suffocating shadow of their family's shame. They were loyal Paladins who tried to live down the disgrace through quiet, unwavering service. However, they were forever viewed with suspicion, passed over for promotions, and subjected to the quiet condescension of their peers. Their life was a constant, draining effort to prove their worth. This pressure took its toll; they were sent on increasingly dangerous and thankless missions, eventually falling in battle when Garrett was just a boy, leaving him an orphan within the very walls that had judged his family so harshly.
Garrett grew up not as the heir of a noble house, but as "the grandson of the dissenter." He was an outcast among the other children of the Fortress. This environment forged the core of his personality. He became hyper-observant, learning to read the subtle shifts in a room, to analyze who held power, and to anticipate scorn. He developed his sharp, dry wit as a shield—a way to deflect insults and control social situations before they could control him. He learned that rigid adherence to dogma got his family shamed, so he adopted a quiet, internal pragmatism, trusting only in what he could see and analyze for himself.
III. Joining the Order: The Path to Redemption and a Found Family
Garrett’s decision to join the Order was a complex act of defiance and desperation. With no other prospects, it was the only life available to him, but his motivation was twofold. First, it was a burning desire to restore honor to the Alerion name, not by challenging his grandfather's beliefs, but by becoming the most effective, intelligent, and loyal Paladin the Order had ever seen. Second, it was a more subversive goal: to prove, from within the system, that his grandfather's pragmatic approach to protecting people was, in fact, the correct one.
It was in the training yards that his life truly began. He met two other orphans who, like him, were outsiders: Kaelen, the haunted survivor carrying a heavy, unspoken burden, and Serafina, the gentle soul whose innate compassion set her apart. They saw him not as the grandson of a heretic, but as Garrett. For the first time, he found unconditional acceptance and belonging. This bond, forged in shared isolation and the crucible of initiate training, became the unshakable foundation of his life. Kaelen and Serafina were not just his friends; they were his pack, his true house, and his primary affiliation, superseding even his duty to the Order that had wronged his family.
IV. The Incident at the Silent Spire: The Sentinel's Vow
His purpose was violently cemented during the disastrous mission to the Silent Spire. When he was critically wounded and helpless, watching a monster bear down on him, he experienced the absolute terror his grandfather had tried to prevent for others. He was saved only by the two people he trusted most: Kaelen, who unleashed a terrifying, forbidden power from Oathbringer to protect him, and Serafina, who risked everything to heal him.
That day, Garrett's abstract motivations became a concrete, personal vow. He owed them his life. He saw firsthand the dangerous power Kaelen carried and the immense burden of Serafina's compassion. His role was no longer just about restoring a family name; it was about becoming their unwavering sentinel. He would be the tactician who would prevent them from ever being put in such a helpless position again. He would be the shield for Serafina's light and the anchor for Kaelen's darkness. This is why his loyalty is absolute, even when Kaelen's actions frighten him, and why his protective instincts are so fierce. His life's purpose is to ensure his found family never suffers a calamity like the one that shattered his own.
